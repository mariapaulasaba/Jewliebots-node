What are Jewliebots?

Jewliebots introduce software education to teenaged girls in a way that is appealing to them. It is a set of neat jewelry and wearables that girls can program their appearance and behavior in an easy to use web interface.

Jewliebots is a joint effort of Sara Chipps and Maria Paula Saba, who developed it as part of her master thesis at ITP/NYU. They are currently working on new prototypes and looking for investments/ help to make it feasible to large produce them.

Why Jewliebots?

Tech is a male dominated field. Girls are discouraged to follow a tech
career for several reasons, like lack of role models and CS intimidating environments. Computer Sciences jobs are increasing in an incredible rate and women won't be able to compete for these jobs if they don't learn programming. This project's goal is to engage and increase girls interest in the workings of technology by showing them that coding can be also beautiful.

Jewlie #1 - our first prototype!

The first prototype is a 3D printed flexible bracelet, with colored lights, buttons and a motion sensor. The colors change according to the code, that can be customized through a web interface. In this way, girls can easily express themselves through how the jewel looks and reacts to movement and button presses at the same time they learn logics of programming.

Who are Jewliebots designed to?

Jewliebots are aimed at teenaged girls, but anyone can use it!
So far, we have done user tests in the Lower East Side Girls Club and at Eleanor Roosevelt High School, with girls from 14-17 years old, and in both places we got great reception.

Girls could easily adopt the programming interface and expressed great happiness when they see their code happening in the jewel.
